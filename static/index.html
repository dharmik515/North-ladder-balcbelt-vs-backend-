💡 What do these levels mean? (click to expand/collapse)

Every row in your inventory is audited for scan errors, format issues, and inconsistencies — checked against its own fields, the rest of the batch, and the Blackbelt catalog. Each row is then placed into one of four buckets by severity:

🚨 Confirmed Errors (severity: Most Important)
  • How it was decided: the row fails a hard rule — e.g. IMEI has wrong length or fails Luhn, a serial number sits in the IMEI column, two rows claim the same IMEI for different devices, or the brand contradicts the model.
  • Why you can trust it: these are near-certain scan or data-entry mistakes, not judgment calls.
  • What to do: fix at the source. Re-scan the device or correct the field.
⚠ Likely Errors (severity: Important)
  • How it was decided: the row breaks a strong pattern — e.g. storage or color mentioned in the asset label isn't a real configuration for that model in the Blackbelt catalog, or the TAC (first 8 digits of IMEI) disagrees with the rest of the batch of the same model.
  • Why it's not "Confirmed": the rule is strong but not absolute — the catalog could be incomplete, or the batch could be mixed.
  • What to do: analyst should skim each one (5-10 seconds per row) and confirm before correcting.
🔎 Advisory Flags (severity: Review)
  • How it was decided: a weak signal — e.g. a possible IMEI1/IMEI2 pair, an unusual but not impossible value, or a field that's blank where most rows have one.
  • Why it's only advisory: these often turn out to be fine on inspection, but they're worth a sanity check.
  • What to do: spot-check a sample. Do not auto-correct.
✅ Clean Rows
  • How it was decided: the row passed every detection rule — format checks, cross-field consistency, batch-level patterns, and catalog validation.
  • Why this is good: no errors detected in these rows.
  • What to do: nothing — these rows are fine as they are.
